Bean and Cheese Pupusas

Pupusas are one of those foods that once you've had them, you wonder just how you managed to go so long with out them. For a while there was a Honduran restaurant next to my work and I went probably every day for a month.

Ingredients:

  • 3 cups of masa harina
  • Half a teaspoon of salt
  • 1 and a 1/2 cups of warm water
  • 1 cup of shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1 cup of refried beans
  • Oil for sauteeing

Instructions:

  1. Mix the masa, warm water, and salt to make the dough. If the dough feels to dry or if it starts cracking, you can add more water a tablespoon at a time. You should be able to form it into a ball without any cracks if the consistancy is correct. Ince you have a good consistancy, knead for 1 minute and then let it rest for 15 minutes.
  2. Once the dough has rested, coat your hands in a bit of oil or grease and then separate into 8 balls. Flatten the balls into discs about a 1/4" thick then put as much cheese and beans in the center as you can. Then, pinch the dough around the filling leaving no gaps and flatten them into patties.
  3. While working, you can store the already formed pupusas on a plate under a damp towel to prevent them from drying out too much.
  4. Grease a pan with oil and cook the pupusas on medium heat for about 5 minutes a side or until golden brown.
  5. Serve hot with curtido and salsa roja.
